Example 3.1. Cybersecurity Extension

The previous example estimates the probability of a high-impact compromise. However, the final business impact also depends on whether the organization detects and contains the intrusion early.

Two additional factors are added to the model:

  • Internal Detection: there is a 52% chance that the organization detects the malicious activity internally before being notified by an external party. This value is based on Mandiant’s M-Trends 2026 data, which reported that breaches were detected internally in 52% of cases.
  • Early containment: if the intrusion is detected internally, there is a 60% chance that the security team contains the incident before it causes severe operational or data-loss consequences. Containment actions may include disabling the compromised account, revoking active sessions, rotating credentials, isolating affected systems, blocking command-and-control traffic and preserving forensic evidence. This value is a modelling assumption and should be calibrated using incident-response exercises or historical SOC metrics.

With these additions, the expected high-impact incidents per month can be recalculated as: 0.2592 x (1 - 0.312) = 0.1783 incidents per month. This is equivalent to approximately one high-impact incident every five to six months.

This extension makes the model more realistic because the impact of a cybersecurity incident does not only depend on whether the attacker gains access. It also depends on the organization’s ability to detect, investigate and contain the intrusion quickly. Mandiant’s M-Trends 2026 report also reported a global median dwell time of 14 days, showing that attackers may remain inside an environment for days before being detected.

As in the previous example, these values should not be treated as fixed probabilities for every organization. They are parameters of the model. In a real deployment, they should be replaced or refined using internal detection metrics, mean time to detect, mean time to respond, incident-response simulations and historical incident data.
