Example 1. In-line quality control

A factory produces screws on an automated production line. The quality control process consists of two stages: first, an automatic visual inspection (using a camera), and then—only if the camera detects a defect—a manual inspection by an operator. Historical data shows that:

  • 5% of the screws are defective.
  • The camera correctly identifies 90% of the defective screws (true positive rate).
  • The camera incorrectly flags 10% of the non-defective screws as defective (false positive rate).
